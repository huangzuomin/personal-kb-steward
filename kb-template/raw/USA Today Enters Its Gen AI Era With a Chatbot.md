The publishing company behind USA Today and 220 other publications is today rolling out a -like tool called DeeperDive that can converse with readers, summarize insights from its journalism, and suggest new content from across its sites.

“Visitors now have a trusted AI answer engine on our platform for anything they want to engage with, anything they want to ask,” said Mike Reed, CEO of Gannett and the USA Today Network, at the [WIRED AI Power Summit](https://www.wired.com/story/wired-ai-power-summit/) in New York, an event that brought together voices from the tech industry, politics, and the world of media, “and it is performing really great.”

Most publishers have a fraught relationship with AI, as the chatbots that [trained on their content](https://pressgazette.co.uk/comment-analysis/human-traffic-down-bot-traffic-up-tollbit/#:~:text=AI%20bots%20appear%20to%20be,AI%20bots%20scraping%20publisher%20websites.&text=Human%20traffic%20to%20publisher%20websites,AI%20licensing%20start%2Dup%20Tollbit.) are now summarizing it and [eating the traffic](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c0mlvryx0exo) that search engines used to send them.

Reed said that Google’s AI Overview feature has dramatically cut traffic to publishers across the industry. “We are watching the same movie as everyone else is watching,” Reed said ahead of today’s announcement. “We can see some risk in the future to any content distribution model that is based primarily on SEO optimization.”

Like other publishers, Gannett has signed some deals with AI companies, including Amazon and Perplexity, to license its content. The company actively [blocks the web scrapers](https://www.usatoday.com/bot-detection) that crawl websites in order to steal content.

DeeperDive represents a bet that harnessing the same generative technology could help publishers capture readers attention by engaging with them in new ways.

The tool replaces a conventional search box and automatically suggests questions that readers might want to ask. For example, today it offers as one prompt, “How does Trump’s Fed policy affect the economy?”

DeeperDive generates a short answer to the query along with relevant stories from across the USA Today network. Reed says it is crucial that DeeperDive bases its output on factually correct information and does not draw from opinion pieces. “We only look at our real journalism,” he says.

![The interface of DeeperDive on the homepage of USA Today](https://media.wired.com/photos/68c816921d0720a3e0474b2d/master/w_120,c_limit/image)

The interface of DeeperDive on the homepage of USA Today

Reed adds that his company hopes that the tool will also reveal more about readers’ interests. “That can help us from a revenue standpoint,” he said.

DeeperDive was developed by the advertising company Taboola. Adam Singolda, Taboola’s CEO, says his firm developed DeeperDive by fine-tuning several open source models.

Singolda says DeeperDive benefits from data gathered from across its own network of more than 600 million daily readers across around 11,000 publishers. He says the tool “grounds every answer in articles retrieved from our publisher partners and requires sentence-level citations to those sources” and will avoid generating an output if information from two sources seems to conflict.

Gannett’s CEO Reed said ahead of today’s event that, together with Taboola, his firm is interested in exploring agentic tools for readers’ shopping decisions. “Our audiences have a higher intent to purchase to begin with,” he says. “That's really the next step here.”

拥有《今日美国》及其他220家出版物的出版公司，今日推出了一款名为DeeperDive的类似聊天机器人工具。该工具能够与读者对话，总结其新闻报道中的见解，并推荐其旗下网站上的新内容。

根尼特（Gannett）和《今日美国》网络（USA Today Network）的首席执行官迈克·里德（Mike Reed）在纽约举行的[WIRED AI Power Summit](https://www.wired.com/story/wired-ai-power-summit/)（一个汇集了科技界、政界和媒体界人士的活动）上表示：“现在，访问者可以在我们的平台上拥有一个值得信赖的AI问答引擎，可以就任何他们感兴趣或想提问的内容进行互动，而且它的表现非常出色。”

大多数出版商与AI的关系紧张，因为那些[利用其内容进行训练](https://pressgazette.co.uk/comment-analysis/human-traffic-down-bot-traffic-up-tollbit/#:~:text=AI%20bots%20appear%20to%20be,AI%20bots%20scraping%20publisher%20websites.&text=Human%20traffic%20to%20publisher%20websites,AI%20licensing%20start%2Dup%20Tollbit.)的聊天机器人现在正在总结这些内容，并[吞噬](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c0mlvryx0exo)过去由搜索引擎带来的流量。

里德表示，谷歌的AI概览功能已大幅削减了整个行业出版商的流量。在今日的发布会前，里德说：“我们正在看和大家一样的电影。我们预见到，未来任何主要依赖搜索引擎优化（SEO）的内容分发模式都存在一定风险。”

与其他出版商一样，根尼特已与包括亚马逊和Perplexity在内的AI公司签署了一些协议，以授权其内容。该公司积极[阻止](https://www.usatoday.com/bot-detection)那些抓取网站以窃取内容的网络爬虫。

DeeperDive代表着一种尝试，即利用相同的生成式技术，通过新的互动方式帮助出版商吸引读者的注意力。

该工具取代了传统的搜索框，并自动建议读者可能想提出的问题。例如，今天它提供的一个提示是：“特朗普的联邦储备政策如何影响经济？”

DeeperDive会生成对查询的简短回答，并附带《今日美国》网络中的相关报道。里德表示，DeeperDive的关键在于其输出基于事实正确的信息，而非观点文章。“我们只关注我们真正的新闻报道，”他说。

![The interface of DeeperDive on the homepage of USA Today](https://media.wired.com/photos/68c816921d0720a3e0474b2d/master/w_120,c_limit/image)
《今日美国》主页上的DeeperDive界面

里德补充说，他的公司希望该工具也能揭示更多读者的兴趣。“这能从收入角度帮助我们，”他说。

DeeperDive由广告公司Taboola开发。Taboola首席执行官亚当·辛戈尔达（Adam Singolda）表示，他的公司通过微调多个开源模型开发了DeeperDive。

辛戈尔达表示，DeeperDive受益于其自身网络中从约11,000家出版商、超过6亿日常读者那里收集的数据。他说，该工具“将每个答案都基于从我们的出版商合作伙伴那里检索到的文章，并要求对这些来源进行句子级别的引用”，如果来自两个来源的信息似乎冲突，它将避免生成输出。
